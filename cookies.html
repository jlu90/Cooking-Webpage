Easy Chocolate Chip Cookies



Whenever I am feeling stressed or down, there is nothing that I love to do more than bake! Many recipes call for 1 cup of butter, but in the midst of the global pandemic, butter can be hard to come by. This recipe by Sweetest Menu is perfect because it satisfies my cookie cravings without using all of the butter in my fridge. They're soft and chewy on the inside with a nice crunch on the bottom. Comfort in cookie form. :)

Basic Information:
  • Makes: 16 cookies
  • Total Cook Time: 2 hours (includes prep and baking)
  • Recipe based on these cookies by Sweetest Menu
What You Will Need:
  • 1/2 cup salted butter (softened)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 gup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
How to Make It:
  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until it resembles a smooth paste
  3. Mix in the egg and vanilla extract
  4. Sprinkle in the baking soda and stir until well-combined
  5. Add the flour in small chunks and combine until the dough is smooth
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips
  7. Place dough in the fridge for 30 minutes
  8. Remove dough from fridge and roll dough into 1-2" balls
  9. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper
  10. Place dough balls on the baking sheet, approximately 2" apart
  11. Bake cookies for 10-15 minutes until just browned on the bottom
  12. Enjoy! :)
